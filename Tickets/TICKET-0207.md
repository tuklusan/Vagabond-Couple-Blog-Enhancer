# TICKET-0207: [context_extractor.py/nodes.py] Temporal validity -- inserted facts must not postdate the trip
Status: Closed
Priority: High
Type: Enhancement
Created: 2026-07-05
Description: User rule (2026-07-05, during the peru-2023 case -- a December 2022 trip): the writer/reviewer models are trained well AFTER the narrated trips, so they know how events evolved since (the 2023 course of the Peruvian protests, later construction/renaming/statistics). A generated fact can therefore be TRUE today yet an anachronism in the narrative -- something the narrator could not have known at trip time. The plain "is this fact true?" review criterion cannot catch this; nothing in the pipeline enforced it.
Steps to Reproduce: N/A -- direct user instruction; risk demonstrated by the model's knowledge postdating every test post.
Notes: Implemented in two parts. (1) `context_extractor.extract_trip_timeframe(html)`: deterministically derives when the trip happened from the post's own dates. Heuristics hardened against real posts: travel posts are dense with HISTORICAL dates ('Alaska joined the union in January 1959'), so a naive majority vote picks the history; day-bearing dates alone also misfire when the trip's dates are never day-written. Final rule: trip year = max(day-bearing-date years UNION years mentioned >=2x), months from that year's mentions. Verified: peru -> 'January 2023' (trip end -- the correct anachronism bound), alaska -> '2014' (the author's own evident knowledge horizon: the post itself references 2014; overshoot never breaks the guard, undershoot -- which WOULD wrongly reject valid facts -- is prevented by the max()). Stored as context['trip_timeframe']; empty when a post carries no dates (rule then simply absent). (2) `nodes.temporal_rule(context, for_reviewer)`: a shared clause threaded into the writer AND reviewer prompts of every fact-INSERTING node -- step9f_factoid, step13_separator, step10_journey_significance, step12_resolve -- writer: 'every fact must have been true AS OF {timeframe}'; reviewer: temporal validity is part of the FACTS criterion, FAIL post-trip facts even if currently true. Also (same commit, step13 tuning from the peru run's mass escalations): the separator writer now explicitly prefers well-documented guidebook-grade facts over obscure specifics, and its reviewer prefers REVISE-with-a-concrete-steer over ESCALATE when the subject is well-known -- raising the certification rate within the web-less reviewer's verification ceiling. Tests: test_trip_timeframe_extraction (diary/span/bare-year/no-dates + prompt threading both directions + absent-when-undated). Related memory: no-facts-beyond-trip-dates.
